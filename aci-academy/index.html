Académie ACI

ACI 学院

Develop the five creative capacities of Agentic Creative Intelligence to ensure your engagement with AI is meaningful. Assess where you stand, be inspired by a role model, and generate a personalised Creative Council to guide your creative collaboration with any AI.

Développez les cinq capacités créatives de l'Intelligence Créative Agentique pour garantir que votre engagement avec l'IA soit significatif. Évaluez votre niveau, inspirez-vous d'un modèle, et générez un Conseil Créatif personnalisé pour guider votre collaboration créative avec toute IA.

培养能动创造智能的五种创造力，确保您与AI的互动富有意义。评估现状，从榜样中获得启发，生成个人创意理事会来指导您与任何AI的创造性协作。

AI makes prompting frictionless, and cognitive retreat effortless. The ACI Academy aims to reclaim human agency with practices for the agentic era.

L'IA rend le prompting sans friction et le retrait cognitif sans effort. L'Académie ACI vise à reconquérir l'agentivité humaine avec des pratiques pour l'ère agentique.

AI 让提示变得无摩擦，让认知退缩变得毫不费力。ACI 学院旨在通过智能体时代的实践重塑人类能动性。

Stage 2 Étape 2 第二阶段

Pick Your Role Model Choisissez votre modèle 选择您的榜样

Study practitioners who demonstrate ACI at its most developed — from Miyazaki to Miles. Choose the one whose capabilities you want to develop. Some practitioners appear across multiple archetypes — ACI capacity is not archetype-locked. Étudiez des praticiens qui démontrent l'ACI à son plus haut niveau — de Miyazaki à Miles. Choisissez celui dont les capacités vous inspirent. Certains praticiens apparaissent dans plusieurs archétypes. 研究展现最成熟ACI的实践者——从宫崎骏到迈尔斯。选择您想要发展的能力方向。部分实践者跨越多个原型——ACI能力不受原型限制。

Showing role models for: Modèles pour : 显示原型的榜样：

Santiago Calatrava
Built Arts · Engineering
Trained as both architect and structural engineer, Calatrava draws every day to maintain the somatic connection between hand, eye, and structural intuition. His tools extend his engagement with physical forces rather than replacing it.
Hayao Miyazaki
Film · Animation
Miyazaki storyboards by hand before any dialogue is written, insisting that embodied drawing — not scripting — is where the story lives. Studio Ghibli's resistance to digital shortcuts is a deliberate stance on creative integrity.
Miles Davis
Music · Mentoring
Davis assembled bands as developmental incubators — Coltrane, Hancock, Shorter all emerged from his ensembles. He knew when to direct, when to withhold, and when to reinvent entirely. Each era was a new creative ecology.
David Bowie
Music · Performance · Culture
Bowie spanned painting, kabuki, internet ventures, and financial innovation across a single career. He built collaborative ecologies with Eno, Visconti, and Fripp — each collaborator's entry point complementing his own.
Pina Bausch
Dance · Theatre
Bausch's dancers drew on their own lived experience as choreographic material. Tanztheater was not performance of a script but embodied research — the body as the primary instrument of creative knowledge.
Luis Barragán
Architecture
Barragán treated colour and light as spatial materials with emotional weight. His architecture demonstrates that aesthetic awareness is not decoration — it is the primary structure of how a space makes you feel.
Holly Herndon
Music · AI
Herndon trained an AI on her own vocal tradition — liturgical singing, choral practice — creating a collaborator that extends rather than replaces her embodied voice. A working model of human-AI creative partnership.
Marina Abramović
Performance Art
Abramović made duration and physical endurance the medium itself. Her performances demand sustained presence from both artist and audience — an extended engagement that no shortcut can replicate.
David Adjaye
Architecture · Culture
Adjaye draws on African spatial traditions, material culture, and communal memory to shape buildings that carry meaning beyond function. His work demonstrates aesthetic awareness rooted in cultural specificity.
Sebastião Salgado
Photography · Environmentalism
Salgado spent years embedded in the communities he photographed — workers, migrants, landscapes. His expressive influence comes from sustained engagement, not from capturing moments but from inhabiting them.
Olafur Eliasson
Art · Technology
Eliasson runs his studio as a laboratory where technology serves perceptual experience. Every installation is an experiment in how people sense space, light, and atmosphere — innovation grounded in embodied perception.
Bill Evans
Music · Recording
Evans described his approach to improvisation through sumi-e painting — you must know the gesture before the brush touches paper. His recordings with Scott LaFaro demonstrate “sympathy to bend” in real-time collaboration.
Seymour Papert
Computing · Education
Papert built Logo and the concept of constructionism — the idea that people learn by making things in the world, not by receiving information. His tools extended children's engagement with mathematical thinking through play.
Mariana Mazzucato
Economics · Innovation Policy
Mazzucato reframed who creates value in innovation systems, challenging the assumption that only the private sector takes creative risk. Her work reshapes how institutions understand their own adaptive intention.
Paulo Freire
Education · Social Change
Freire rejected the “banking model” of education where knowledge is deposited into passive recipients. His pedagogy of the oppressed is a blueprint for cultivating agency — the learner as active creator of meaning.
Maria Montessori
Education · Child Development
Montessori designed learning environments where the child's hands, senses, and movement drive understanding — not instruction. Her method is a century-old argument that embodied engagement is the foundation of intelligence.
bell hooks
Education · Cultural Criticism
hooks treated the classroom as a space of radical possibility where engaged pedagogy demands the teacher's full presence. Teaching is not transmission but a practice of freedom — adaptive intention embodied in relationship.
Ai Weiwei
Art · Activism
Ai Weiwei uses creativity as the power to reject the status quo and reveal what systems would prefer to hide. His expressive influence operates at the intersection of art, architecture, and political disruption.
Wangari Maathai
Environmentalism · Civic Leadership
Maathai's Green Belt Movement connected tree planting to women's empowerment, democratic governance, and ecological restoration. She demonstrated that creative conviction at scale requires working through communities, not above them.
Muhammad Yunus
Social Enterprise · Finance
Yunus built microfinance by staying immersed in the lives of people the formal system excluded. Grameen Bank emerged from extended engagement with a problem — not from optimising existing models but from seeing what they missed.
Jacinda Ardern
Political Leadership
Ardern led through crisis by combining empathetic presence with decisive action — holding the ecology of a nation's grief, safety, and collective identity together. Leadership as the integration of all four capacities under pressure.
Jony Ive
Design · Industry
Ive coordinated engineers, materials scientists, and interaction designers toward products where every decision served a coherent sensory experience. His leadership held aesthetic awareness and adaptive intention in constant tension.
Peter Senge
Systems Thinking · Organisational Learning
Senge's learning organisation is a framework for leaders who coordinate others' creative work. His five disciplines describe the conditions under which collective intelligence develops rather than fragments.
