What happens when the world's largest power system and the world's largest AI deployment program are run by the same planner?

On May 26, China's National Energy Administration convened a national "AI plus energy" meeting in Shenzhen and published its first list of 51 high-value scenarios for fusing AI into the energy system, from AI-generated grid planning to virtual power plant operation and vehicle-to-grid coordination. Twenty-five energy companies signed an initiative opening those scenarios to AI developers. The NEA also put a number on the other side of the ledger: China's computing centers consumed 170 billion kWh in 2025, 1.6 percent of national electricity, across 42 ten-thousand-GPU AI clusters.

Why this matters in China. The scenario list converts policy into procurement. State-owned grid and energy giants execute NEA mandates quickly, and a 2025 implementation plan already commits to at least five specialized energy AI models and ten replicable demonstration projects by 2027. With 3.99 billion kW of generating capacity, including 1.25 billion kW of solar, China is the largest sandbox on earth for AI-run grid operations.

Global impact. The scenarios, standards, and demonstration projects that emerge will travel with Chinese grid equipment and EV charging infrastructure into export markets. Western utilities still treat AI as a pilot-stage add-on. China is standardizing it as core grid infrastructure.

Monday morning action. Map the 51 scenarios against your own operations and ask which ones your current vendors can match. Track which Chinese pilots win state funding, since those will define the cost benchmarks for AI-run grid services within three years.

Also today: Tencent is testing an AI agent embedded directly in WeChat, built to execute tasks across millions of mini programs for 1.4 billion users. Regulatory review could begin this month.
