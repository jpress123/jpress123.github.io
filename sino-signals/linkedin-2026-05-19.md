What does a 20 billion US dollar pre-IPO valuation for a single AI video unit tell you about where this market is heading?

Hong Kong-listed Kuaishou Technology disclosed on May 12 that its board is evaluating a restructuring of Kling AI assets and business. Caixin and South China Morning Post reporting on May 13 places the pre-IPO target at a 20 billion US dollar valuation and a roughly 2 billion US dollar primary raise, ahead of a separate listing in 2027. That valuation is about 70 percent of Kuaishou's own market capitalisation, which is why the parent stock jumped close to ten percent on the news.

Kling closed 2025 at 1.04 billion yuan in revenue, crossed a 500 million US dollar annualised run rate by April 2026, and now counts more than 60 million global users, 600 million generated videos, and 30,000 enterprise clients on its API.

Why this matters in China. Kling sits inside Kuaishou's short-video stack of more than 700 million monthly active users, which feeds both training data and a built-in distribution funnel that Sora and Veo 3 cannot match. The round will fund GPU procurement at a moment when ByteDance's Volcano Engine token economy has pushed daily video-generation volume past 120 trillion tokens across the Chinese cloud stack.

Global impact. The public-comparable for OpenAI Sora and Google Veo is now a Chinese listed entity, not a Western private one. Morgan Stanley's 40x forward-ARR comp puts a 2027 Kling closer to 52 billion US dollars, which will reset comparable rounds for Runway, Pika, and Luma in the second half of 2026.
