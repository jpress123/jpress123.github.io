When does an AI-generated movie stop being a demo and start being product?

On May 13 at the Marché du Film in Cannes, the China Film+ initiative previewed two milestone titles. The Reunion Journey, a 94-minute panda-sibling fable produced by China Media Group, was promoted as China's first AIGC animated feature. Legends of the South, produced by Wing Sight, was billed as the country's first AIGC documentary. AI compressed The Reunion Journey from a typical three to five year production cycle into about one year.

The May 14 China Night gala, co-hosted by the China Film Administration, China Film Group Corporation, the China Film Producers Association, and Wing Sight, paired the screenings with a Creative Sovereignty of Filmmakers in the AI Era panel. Director Li Shaohong and Anton Capital's Sébastien Raybaud debated the question, when AI intervenes, who is the author?

Why China can scale this fast: China's AI-animated drama market reached 2.8 billion US dollars by the end of 2025. Wing Sight founder Tina Jia projects the broader AI film sector will reach 100 billion yuan within five years. State-aligned production, China Media Group on the documentary side, Wing Sight on the production side, gives domestic AIGC features a distribution path through the China Film Pavilion that independent Western studios lack.

Global impact: Long-form AIGC features at Cannes change the conversation from AI as back-office assist to AI as a finished theatrical product courting international distribution. Co-production negotiations now begin with a cost base Hollywood mid-budget animation cannot match.

Monday morning: If you run animation or documentary, commission a 90-minute AIGC pilot inside one production cycle and benchmark cost and quality against your live workflow. If you set guild or union policy, draft a credit and revenue framework for AI-generated long-form before festival precedent sets it for you.
