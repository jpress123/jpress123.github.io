# Sino-Signals | June 12, 2026 — China Starts Exporting Tokens While Its Top Lab Bets Beyond Them

**Subtitle:** A Guangdong port city turns green electricity into AI exports, while Beijing's founding AI lab declares the language-model era over.

China's AI story moved on two fronts this week, one commercial and one scientific. A coastal city proved that AI inference can be packaged and sold across borders like any other export, and the research institute that started China's large-model era announced it has already moved past large language models entirely.

## Beijing's BAAI Declares World Models the Next AI Battleground

The Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence, whose Wudao 1.0 opened China's large-model era in March 2021, told Beijing News on June 12 that large language models are no longer its main research direction. Director Wang Zhongyuan is betting the institute on world models, systems that predict the next physical state instead of the next token. BAAI shipped its Emu3 and Emu3.5 multimodal world models in 2025, followed by Physis and RoboBrain Orca this year. The announcement set the agenda for the 2026 BAAI Conference, which opened June 12 in Beijing with over 200 experts and more than 40 AI company CEOs.

BAAI has converted research bets into industries before. Its Wudao program incubated Zhipu AI and ModelBest and trained talent like Moonshot founder Yang Zhilin. The institute hands matured fields to companies and moves on, and it staffs for the long game, with a fourth-year undergraduate leading its behavior world model center.

World models are the same frontier where Yann LeCun, Fei-Fei Li, and Google DeepMind are placing bets. Wang compares the field to language models in 2012 and names data scarcity as the core bottleneck, with four technical routes still competing. If BAAI's track record repeats, today's research direction becomes China's robotics and autonomous-driving software stack within five years.

## Shantou Turns Cheap Green Power Into AI Token Exports

Shantou, Guangdong has built China's first compliant channel for exporting AI inference as a service. Overseas requests travel through submarine cables to a physically isolated compute zone, run on domestic models like DeepSeek and Qwen, and return abroad with per-token billing. Daily volume jumped from 100 million tokens in late April to over 10 billion by late May, and the first commercial loop closed May 18 when a Singapore user's AI toy called roughly 100,000 tokens billed at 2 yuan per million. One 0.5-yuan kilowatt-hour of electricity becomes 11 yuan of exported token value, a 22-fold uplift.

The advantages stack. Shantou lands over half of China's submarine cable export bandwidth with 32.7-millisecond latency to Singapore, plans offshore wind capacity exceeding 53 percent of Guangdong's total, holds a state-approved data processing trade pilot, and offers 30 percent compute subsidies. Chenghai, China's toy capital, is converting itself into an AI toy export base.

The model routes around chip export controls and data-sovereignty objections at once. The chips never leave China, the foreign data never enters China's domestic internet, and the service crosses the border at one-third to one-half the price of international platforms, with reported customer retention above 70 percent. Southeast Asia gets frontier-adjacent AI at commodity prices.
