What if the grid planning study that takes your utility 18 months took 10 minutes?

At the World AI Conference in Shanghai this week, China Southern Power Grid demonstrated exactly that. Its MegaWatt Yunrui planning agent generated a complete distribution-network plan, risk assessment and grid topology included, in 10 minutes on stage. The demo was named one of the conference's Top 10 Signature Exhibits, the only central state-owned enterprise project on the list among more than 1,100 exhibitors. Behind it sits what CSG calls China's first industry-level platform coordinating electricity, carbon, and computing, built on a proprietary power-sector foundation model and an operating system managing hundreds of millions of grid devices.

Why this matters in China: coordination between computing infrastructure and power systems entered the Government Work Report for the first time this year. That is a national mandate, and CSG serves five provinces. A state utility can deploy this architecture across an entire regional grid by decision, not by market adoption.

The global stakes are direct. Western utilities treat AI data centers as a grid burden, with interconnection queues stretching years in the US and Europe. China is inverting the frame, treating computing load, carbon accounting, and grid dispatch as one co-optimized system. If it works at CSG scale, it becomes the reference architecture for absorbing AI power demand while holding carbon targets.

Monday morning: if your operations depend on grid capacity, from data centers to EV fleets, ask your utility partners whether their planning is AI-assisted and how fast an interconnection study can actually run. Benchmark that 10-minute demo against your current timeline, then press regulators on what closes the gap.
