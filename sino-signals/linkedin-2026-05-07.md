What happens when a state utility wires a 500 MW solar plant directly into an AI data center cluster, bypassing the grid?

On May 2, China Datang Corporation commissioned the photovoltaic phase of its Zhongwei Cloud Base in Ningxia, the first utility-scale "computing-electricity coordination" (算电协同) project in the country. A 1.5 GW wind farm follows in September, with energy storage stitching the two together. First-phase capex is 8.7 billion yuan. Dedicated transmission lines push solar power straight into data center halls, sidestepping the main grid entirely. Annual demand at the cluster runs 2.29 billion kWh.

Why China can scale it. The Eastern Data, Western Computing initiative has already pulled more than 80 percent of national intelligent compute capacity into eight western hubs. New hub data centers face an 80 percent green electricity threshold. Ningxia's wind and solar resources, paired with State Grid backing for the 算电协同 model, give Datang a direct template to clone across Inner Mongolia, Gansu, and Guizhou. Jinko Power has already announced a parallel 1 GW direct-connect deployment.

Global impact. Direct renewable-to-data-center pairing at this scale lands roughly two years ahead of comparable utility-scale builds planned in the United States and Europe. The unit economics shift from hyperscaler power purchase agreements to grid-bypass infrastructure owned by state utilities. Both the carbon profile and the marginal cost of training runs compress sharply. Western cloud providers benchmarking against Chinese inference pricing now face a 2027 scenario where Chinese AI compute runs on dedicated zero-marginal-cost solar.
