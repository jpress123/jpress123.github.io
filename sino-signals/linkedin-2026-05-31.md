What if US export controls did not slow China's AI stack but split it from the global one?

On May 25, 2026, Huawei semiconductor leader He Tingbo presented the Tau (τ) Scaling Law at the IEEE International Symposium on Circuits and Systems in Shanghai. The proposal is to optimise chips for time, the signal propagation delay across devices and systems, rather than for transistor size. The accompanying LogicFolding architecture folds and stacks logic circuits vertically and, at fixed process nodes, delivers a 55 percent transistor density increase, 41 percent performance-core energy efficiency gain and 30 percent wiring-length reduction. The fall 2026 Kirin SoC will be the first commercial implementation at a 3.1 GHz performance core. The 2031 target is 1.4 nanometre equivalent density on legally available manufacturing equipment.

The single announcement is not the story. The story is convergence.

Today, Robert Wu's Baiguan essay maps the galapagosization of Chinese tech, where Huawei's hardware track and DeepSeek's software track now design around each other. DeepSeek V4, released April 24, 2026, cuts per-token inference FLOPs to 27 percent of V3.2 and KV cache to 10 percent at the 1 million token context window. The model was tuned for Huawei Ascend SuperNode clusters, not NVIDIA GPUs. ByteDance has reportedly committed over 5.7 billion US dollars to Huawei Ascend procurement in 2026, the largest known non-NVIDIA AI accelerator order. The hardware, the model, the platform demand and the EDA toolchain are all on one side of the line, and they all run without a US export licence.

Three Monday morning moves. If you set semiconductor or AI procurement strategy, model the gain or loss from a two-stack world before your next capex cycle. If you sell into China, decide whether your 2027 roadmap aligns with the bespoke Huawei toolchain or stays in the TSMC-Intel-NVIDIA orbit. If you write technology export policy, draft your position on whether containment is achieving its stated goal or expediting the very divergence it was designed to prevent.
