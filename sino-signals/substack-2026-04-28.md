## AGIBOT G2 Humanoids Run Live Tablet Production Line at Longcheer in World's First Embodied AI Mass-Production Deployment

On April 15, Shanghai humanoid robotics maker AGIBOT and contract manufacturer Longcheer Technology announced live deployment of AGIBOT G2 humanoids on Longcheer's tablet production line, the first paid embodied AI mass-production contract in consumer electronics. The G2 robots run Multimedia Integrated Testing stations alongside human operators, performing precision pick-and-place at 310 units per hour with 19 to 20 second cycle times. Both companies report operation rates above 99 percent across more than 140 hours of continuous run time. AGIBOT plans to scale to 100 G2 units by Q3 2026 and extend deployments into automotive, semiconductor, and energy plants.

China can move at this pace because Longcheer is one of the world's largest contract manufacturers for smartphones and tablets, supplying global brands at volume. AGIBOT bypassed pilot and went directly to a paid customer line. China produced 143,608 industrial robots in the first two months of 2026 (up 31.1 percent year over year), and industrial enterprise adoption of large models or AI agents climbed from 9.6 percent in 2024 to 47.5 percent in 2025. The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology's January AI Plus Manufacturing action plan targets 500 typical scenarios by 2027, and Shanghai is integrating AI across 3,000 manufacturing enterprises.

For Western leaders, Figure, 1X, Apptronik, and Tesla Optimus have published staged demos while AGIBOT is publishing operations data. The benchmark shifts from whether humanoids can perform precision tasks to what cycle time, success rate, and cost per task each platform delivers in production. Monday morning, ask your robotics vendor for hourly throughput and uninterrupted operation duration on a real customer line, and reprice labor-intensive consumer-electronics bids accordingly.

## Beijing Film Academy Publishes China AI Film and TV Development Report Documenting Six Proven Commercial Models

On April 17, the China AI Film and TV Development Report (2025 to 2026) was released at the Beijing International Film Festival, produced by the Beijing Film Academy's Beijing Film and TV Arts Research Base in partnership with the National Film Think Tank. The cross-institution research team includes Beijing Film Academy, Tsinghua University, Central University of Finance and Economics, and Beijing University of Science and Technology. The report concludes AI now covers the full film and TV pipeline (ideation, shooting, post-production, marketing, operations) with six commercial models initially proven, audience monthly compound growth at 12 percent, and AI-generated video and audio output exceeding 20 billion clips in 2025, more than 14 times the 2024 volume.

The audience economics support fast scaling. The same week's China Internet Audiovisual Development Research Report puts the network audiovisual user base at 1.099 billion, with daily per-user time on micro-dramas reaching 129 minutes (up 28.4 percent year over year, ahead of long-form video). Of the top 100 globally downloaded apps in 2025, 31 came from Chinese developers, and roughly half of those were micro-drama apps. The animation micro-drama market alone grew to 18.98 billion RMB in 2025, up 276.3 percent.

For Western leaders, Hollywood and ad agencies have treated AI video as a tool for trailers and marketing assets. Chinese studios are treating AI as the production stack for short-form content sold to global audiences at scale.
