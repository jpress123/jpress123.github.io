# Sino-Signals | July 7, 2026 — The Nvidia-Free Frontier and the Two-Week Factory Brain

*A food delivery giant trains a trillion-parameter model on Chinese chips alone, and a startup sells factories guaranteed results instead of consulting hours.*

Two announcements this week mark the same shift from different ends of China's AI stack. At the top, Meituan proved that frontier-scale training no longer requires a single Nvidia chip. At the factory floor, Basepoint Origin is turning months-long customization projects into two-week deliveries with contractual guarantees. Both stories are about removing dependencies, on foreign hardware in one case, on human expert teams in the other.

## Meituan Trains a Trillion-Parameter Model Without a Single Nvidia Chip

Meituan released LongCat-2.0 on June 30, a 1.6-trillion-parameter mixture-of-experts model with about 48 billion active parameters and a one-million-token context window, pretrained from scratch on more than 30 trillion tokens using only domestic AI chips. Media reports put the training cluster at roughly 50,000 accelerators, which the technical community identifies as Huawei Ascend 910C, though Meituan itself names only "domestic AI ASIC superpods." Before launch, the model ran anonymously on OpenRouter as "Owl Alpha" and climbed to the global top three by total usage, taking second place in monthly Claude Code calls. Days after launch, Meituan released the weights and inference code under the MIT license.

China can scale this because the ranking came from real developer workloads rather than benchmark submissions, and because Meituan prices access at 9.9 yuan for 50 million tokens, backed by 26 billion yuan of 2025 R&D spending, up 23.5 percent year over year. Its delivery, in-store, and rider-dispatch operations generate agent training data at a volume few companies anywhere possess.

Globally, LongCat-2.0 is the first trillion-parameter model to complete the full pretraining-to-inference loop on Chinese hardware, demonstrating that US export controls no longer cap frontier-scale training for at least one Chinese lab. Together with Zhipu's MIT-licensed GLM-5.2, open Chinese models now advance on capability and compute independence at once.

Monday morning: retire any planning assumption that chip controls hold Chinese frontier training below the trillion-parameter scale, and benchmark LongCat-2.0 on agentic coding workloads this week.

## Basepoint Origin Replaces Hundred-Person Factory Consulting Teams With AI

Basepoint Origin, founded by former Huawei Cloud AI CTO and 01.AI co-founder Dai Zonghong, closed three funding rounds totaling several hundred million yuan within six months, 36Kr reported on June 26, with backers including CAS-affiliated Guoke Investment and Shanghai Semiconductor Industry Investment. Its "full-element large model," which Dai calls an industrial world model, learns a digital twin of a plant directly from raw logs and ERP records, then hands line workers precise operating instructions through a simple app. Orders doubled in six months, contract values rose an order of magnitude, and deployments span more than ten industries including metallurgy, chemicals, semiconductors, and textiles.

China can scale this because customized industrial AI traditionally meant hundreds of consultants on site for months, while Basepoint delivers comparable projects with roughly one person in two weeks. The eight-ministry AI Plus Manufacturing plan targets 1,000 high-level industrial agents by 2027, and IDC finds the share of Chinese industrial firms using large models or agents jumped from 9.6 percent in 2024 to 47.5 percent in 2025.

Globally, Basepoint sells contractual guarantees where Western vendors sell dashboards, with one process segment showing a two to three fold gain on a key metric and annual savings above ten million yuan. Its target industries sit upstream of global EV, battery, and electronics supply chains.

Monday morning: press your industrial software vendors to write a guaranteed business metric into the contract, and treat refusal as information.
