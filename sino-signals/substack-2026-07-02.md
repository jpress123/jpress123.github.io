## China Opens First AI Data Center Running Entirely on Green Power

China's first AI data center supplied entirely through a direct green electricity connection began operations in Zhongwei, Ningxia on June 26, 2026. China Telecom Ningxia Branch built it to a wind powered liquid cooling standard, with a direct line to nearby wind and solar and a Power Usage Effectiveness of 1.15, well below conventional data centers. The project anchors the Eastern Data, Western Computing plan that shifts compute to renewable rich western regions.

China can scale this because Zhongwei already hosts 10 large data center parks with roughly 3.31 million standard server racks and six of the top 10 domestic computing providers, giving the green direct supply model an installed base to copy. Abundant Gobi wind and solar let operators sign direct power deals, and national policy targets renewables covering four fifths of AI data center power by 2030, up from about 11 percent in 2023.

Globally, clean electricity is the tightest constraint on AI, and a 1.15 PUE on fully renewable supply resets the benchmark for what a low carbon AI data center can cost. Western operators face grid queues and rising power prices while Chinese cloud and model firms gain a cheaper, lower emission compute base. Monday morning, ask where your AI compute draws power and whether direct renewable supply can match that number before siting the next training cluster.

## UBTECH Launches World's First Mass Produced Full Size Humanoid Robot

UBTECH unveiled the UWORLD U1 Series at its Shenzhen global launch on June 30, 2026, calling it the world's first full size ultra bionic humanoid robot designed for mass production, priced from 119,800 RMB. Cumulative orders passed 13,361 units by launch day across three models, the U1 Lite, U1 Pro, and U1 Ultra. Each robot carries 88 degrees of freedom and an emotion aware language model that reads more than 20 emotional states at over 90 percent accuracy.

China can scale this because UBTECH ranked first globally in full size humanoid robot revenue and sales in 2025 and already runs its industrial Walker S2 line in mass production, so a consumer model moves down a proven supply chain. A domestic stack of actuators, chips, and assembly prices a full size humanoid near a mid range car, and China's more than 90 million adults living alone and 118 million empty nest seniors give the companion and elder care use cases a vast home market.

Globally, a mass produced full size humanoid at 119,800 RMB resets the price expectation for a category where Western rivals still show prototypes, and thousands of orders signal real demand. Firms in elder care, hospitality, and domestic service now face a Chinese supplier setting the reference price.
