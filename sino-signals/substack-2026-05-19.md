## Kuaishou Board Reviews Spinning Off Kling AI for a Pre-IPO Round at 20 Billion USD Valuation and a Roughly 2 Billion USD Raise Ahead of a Separate 2027 Listing

Hong Kong-listed Kuaishou Technology disclosed on May 12, 2026 that its board is evaluating a restructuring of Kling AI assets and business. Caixin Global and South China Morning Post reporting on May 13 placed the pre-IPO target at a 20 billion US dollar valuation, a roughly 2 billion US dollar primary raise, and a separate Hong Kong listing in 2027. Kling closed 2025 at 1.04 billion yuan in revenue, crossed an annualised run rate of 500 million US dollars by April 2026, and counts more than 60 million global users, 600 million generated videos, and 30,000 enterprise clients on its API. The disclosed valuation is roughly 70 percent of Kuaishou's own market capitalisation, which is why the parent stock jumped close to ten percent on the news.

China can scale this because Kling sits inside Kuaishou's short-video stack of more than 700 million monthly active users, feeding both training data and a built-in distribution funnel that Sora and Veo 3 cannot match. The round will fund GPU procurement at a moment when ByteDance's Volcano Engine token economy has pushed daily video-generation volume past 120 trillion tokens across the Chinese cloud stack, and Kling's API pricing already undercuts Western competitors at production volume.

Global impact. A 20 billion US dollar standalone valuation confirms that the public-comparable for OpenAI Sora and Google Veo is now a Chinese listed entity, not a Western private one. Hollywood studios, advertising holding companies, and streaming platforms running Sora and Veo pilots will face a pricing reset once a publicly traded Kling can publish gross margin and ARR every quarter. ## China Datang Commissions a 500 MW Solar PV Plant Hardwired to the Zhongwei Cloud Base, the Country's First Large Scale Green Power Project Built Specifically to Feed a Data Centre Cluster

China Datang Corporation commissioned a 500 MW solar PV plant at the Zhongwei Cloud Base in Ningxia on May 2, 2026, the country's first large-scale green power project built specifically to feed a data centre cluster, per pv magazine on May 4. The plant is paired with a 1.5 GW wind farm targeted for full commissioning in September 2026 and an energy storage block, anchoring the 2 GW phase one of an integrated source-grid-load-storage scheme worth about 8.7 billion yuan, or 1.27 billion US dollars. Four dedicated 110 kV transmission lines carry the green electrons straight to data centre loads, bypassing the public grid, and the 500 MW unit alone is expected to generate about 970 GWh per year, covering roughly half of base demand.

China can scale this because the Zhongwei base sits inside the eight national hubs of the East Data West Compute corridor, with a State Council mandate to push at least 60 percent capacity utilisation and an average PUE below 1.5 across new builds in the 15th Five-Year Plan. Datang can replicate the source-grid-load-storage template across Ningxia, Gansu, Inner Mongolia, and Qinghai because the same state-owned grid operator owns both the wires and the dispatch, and a future 4.6 GW phase two could bring total project capital close to 20 billion yuan.

Global impact. Direct renewables-to-compute supply at gigawatt scale gives Chinese cloud providers a verifiable Scope 2 story that Western hyperscalers still negotiate Power Purchase Agreement by Power Purchase Agreement. The bypass-the-public-grid template will accelerate Middle Eastern and Southeast Asian copies, since Riyadh, Abu Dhabi, and Jakarta are courting Chinese state EPCs for combined solar-and-data-centre builds.
