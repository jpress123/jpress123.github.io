China Netcasting Services Association Q1 2026 Micro-Drama Guidelines Record 128,000 Titles Launched of Which 122,000 Are AI Generated

On April 30, the China Netcasting Services Association released its Q1 2026 Micro-Drama Creation Guidelines at the Micro-Drama Screenwriters Salon in Shanghai. About 128,000 micro-drama titles launched industry-wide in Q1 2026, of which roughly 122,000 were AI generated, a share above 95 percent. During the Spring Festival window, AI titles outnumbered real-human titles 50 to 1, yet real-human titles still pulled 25 times the total plays. On Douyin alone, single-month plays reached almost 75 billion in March, with new-drama share at 64.3 percent. AI manhua and AI simulated-human drama account for 55 percent of Douyin AI inventory, and 50,000 new titles were added in March alone, lifting Q1 AI title supply above 100,000 across the major platforms.

AI now produces 95 percent of micro-drama supply because the cost curve sits near 1,000 yuan per minute and a single studio can ship 20 titles in a day. Douyin, Kuaishou, iQiyi Nado Pro, and Tencent feed the same distribution funnel that converted 12 million weekly viewers into paid micro-drama subscribers across 2025. China Netcasting Services Association numbers now sit alongside National Radio and Television Administration filing data as the operational baseline regulators, advertisers, and platform finance teams consume each quarter.

Western studios sized for 100 to 300 prestige titles per year now face a market where one country ships more AI micro-drama episodes per quarter than the global feature catalogue ever assembled, with cost per finished minute roughly 1/100 of conventional live-action production. Streaming platforms hunting incremental engagement should expect AI micro-drama to migrate into Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and Latin America through Douyin, Kuaishou, ReelShort, and DramaBox by H2 2026, since the 6.5 billion USD overseas micro-drama market projection for 2026 leans on the same AI pipeline.

Monday morning action: if you run content strategy at a Western streaming platform, commission an AI micro-drama pilot with a Chinese studio capable of delivering 60 episodes inside a four-week window and benchmark unit economics against your current scripted slate by August.

Baidu Create 2026 Robin Li Proposes Daily Active Agents DAA as the New AI Era Metric Replacing Token Consumption

At Baidu Create 2026 in Beijing this morning, founder Robin Li proposed Daily Active Agents (DAA) as the new metric for the AI era, framed as the agentic counterpart to Daily Active Users (DAU) in mobile, and argued that token consumption measures input cost rather than delivered output. Baidu paired the DAA framing with the formal launch of Ernie 5.1, which compresses total parameters to roughly one third and activated parameters to roughly one half of comparable models, runs at about 6 percent of typical pretraining cost, and entered LMArena's Search Arena at 1,223 points to claim the China number one slot and global number four. The two-day conference convenes more than 100 enterprises across 20 vertical sessions.

Baidu's Wenxin Yiyan and Qianfan platform already serve more than 700 enterprise customers, and the DAA framing aligns with Alibaba Cloud, ByteDance Volcano Engine, and Tencent Cloud agent platform launches across Q1 2026. By proposing a measurable agent unit, Baidu is offering procurement officers, regulators, and CFOs a denominator that translates AI spending into deployed workflow capacity.

If DAA becomes the operating metric for Chinese cloud disclosure and procurement, Western hyperscaler reporting on tokens served, API calls, and model active parameters loses comparability against Chinese agent KPIs, forcing analyst note rewrites by late 2026. Boards setting AI capital allocation should expect a measurement shift from compute consumed to agents shipped and tasks completed, which favours vendors with integrated agent runtimes over those still selling raw inference.

Monday morning action: ask your CIO to publish a Daily Active Agents target for FY27 alongside token spend, and define the operational definition (which workflows count, what completion threshold) before the next budget review.
