## Geely Unveils 1+2+N Full-Domain AI Architecture with Super Eva Agent and EVA Cab L4 Robotaxi

On April 24, Geely Auto Group attended Auto China 2026 (Beijing Auto Show) for the first time as a direct exhibitor and unveiled the EVA Cab Robotaxi prototype, jointly developed with AFARI Technology and CaoCao Mobility. The architecture under the hood pairs WAM (World Action Model) as the vehicle brain with Super Eva as the central intelligent agent, two domain agents (Smart Egg cockpit and AFARI G-ASD 4.0 driving), and N sub-domain agents covering chassis, powertrain, and body control. EVA Cab carries the world's first 2,160-line digital LiDAR (25.92 million points per second, 600m detection range), three flagship chips (NVIDIA SuperChip, NVIDIA Thor U, Qualcomm Snapdragon 8397) totalling more than 3,000 TOPS, and Geely's mass-production-ready L4 software AFARI G-ASD L4. The CaoCao Mobility customised edition launches commercially in 2027.

Geely sold 3,024,567 vehicles in 2025 with 90% NEV growth, giving the 1+2+N stack an immediate path into a mass installed base. The Xingrui Intelligent Computing Center reached 23.5 EFLOPS in 2025, ranking first among Chinese automakers, and feeds WAM with the 7x parameter and 3x inference frame-rate gains required for L4 deployment. CaoCao Mobility, Geely's ride-hail subsidiary, supplies the deployment channel, and pilot operations are already running in Hangzhou and Suzhou.

Geely is the first global automaker certified to ISO 8800 AI Safety, setting a reference standard for L4 software validation that European and Japanese OEMs will need to match for cross-border deployment. The 1+2+N pattern (one vehicle brain, two domain agents, N sub-agents) gives Western automakers a template to copy or contest. Volkswagen has already licensed XPeng's competing VLA 2.0 stack for its China fleet. Robotaxi capex bets at Waymo, Cruise, and Zoox now face a Chinese OEM moving directly from passenger-car sales into mass-production L4 hardware on a 2027 calendar.

## China Network Audio-Visual Association Reports AI Micro-Dramas at 95% of Q1 2026 Releases

On April 30, 2026, at the Shanghai Spring AI Season micro-drama gathering, the China Network Audio-Visual Association released its Q1 2026 Micro-Drama Creation Guide. Approximately 128,000 micro-drama series went online in Q1, of which roughly 122,000 (more than 95%) were AI-generated. On April 15, ByteDance's Hongguo (红果短剧) committed 500 million yuan in dedicated funds to live-action production. Kuaishou's Inspiration New Era AIGC programme offers up to 90% revenue share and 100 million yuan in traffic credits. Douyin's Manhua Galaxy plan opens 60,000 IPs from Tomato Novels for AI adaptation with a 90% net-revenue share to creators.

China's micro-drama market crossed 50 billion yuan in 2025, and AI production has compressed unit costs from roughly 500,000 yuan per live-action series to under 30,000 yuan per AI series. Hongguo, Tencent, Kuaishou, Youku, and iQiyi run end-to-end stacks that take a script through asset generation, storyboarding, video synthesis, dubbing, editing, and distribution within days. More than ten provinces and cities have layered local subsidy programmes on top of platform incentives, anchoring AI micro-drama inside the cultural-industry plan.

China is the first major market where generative-AI video crossed the 90% threshold inside a mainstream entertainment vertical, ahead of US and European studios still negotiating SAG-AFTRA-style guardrails. Netflix, Amazon Prime, and TikTok face a Chinese export wave with 2026 overseas revenue forecast at 650 million USD. The 50-to-1 viewership advantage of live-action over AI during Spring Festival 2026 (despite a 1-to-50 supply ratio) tells US studios where audience preference still sits, and where AI scales without crowding out human-led work.
