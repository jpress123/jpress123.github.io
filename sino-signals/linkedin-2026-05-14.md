What happens to your storage project economics when the cheapest grid battery on the market no longer needs lithium?

On April 27, CATL signed the world's largest sodium-ion battery order to date, 60 gigawatt-hours to Beijing HyperStrong Technology over three years. CATL, the world's largest EV and storage battery maker, said the contract proves it has cleared the obstacles to mass-producing sodium-ion cells and can now deliver at scale. A senior executive at battery supplier Suzhou Hazardtex called it a "DeepSeek moment" for the global energy storage industry, because sodium replaces costlier lithium and cuts both material cost and manufacturing complexity.

Why this matters in China. Storage construction is scaling specifically to back AI computing and data centers, and sodium-ion strips out the lithium price exposure that has long constrained grid storage economics. HyperStrong is one of China's largest storage integrators, so this moves sodium-ion from pilot lines into a committed three-year industrial pipeline. Chinese producers have separately announced more than 600 GWh of new storage capacity plans in just the first two months of 2026.

Global impact. Sodium-ion at scale resets the floor price for grid storage. That compounds the cost advantage Chinese developers already carry into solar-plus-storage tenders across the Middle East, Southeast Asia, and Europe. Western developers and utilities still pricing on lithium-ion economics will meet Chinese bids built on a cheaper, more abundant chemistry by 2027.

Monday morning. If you procure grid storage, request sodium-ion pricing alongside lithium-ion in your next tender and model the three-year cost curve before you commit to a chemistry. If you invest in battery supply chains, stress-test your lithium exposure against sodium-ion taking 20 to 30 percent of new grid storage by 2028.
