## Cyberspace Administration, NDRC and MIIT Jointly Issue First National Framework for AI Agents With 19 Application Scenarios and Tiered Risk Filing for High-Stakes Sectors

What happened. The Implementation Opinions on the Standardized Application and Innovative Development of Intelligent Agents (智能体规范应用与创新发展实施意见) define AI agents as systems capable of autonomous perception, memory, decision-making, interaction, and execution, and bind them to a four-pillar program covering technical foundations, safety bottom lines, application traction, and innovation ecosystem. The document names 19 typical application scenarios across scientific research, industrial development, consumption stimulus, public well-being, and social governance, with explicit focus on smart manufacturing, energy and resources, transportation, financial services, healthcare, government services, judicial services, and public safety. Developers must clarify decision boundaries, preserve user override on autonomous decisions, and submit agents serving sensitive sectors to filing, testing, and product recall under joint oversight by cyberspace regulators and sector authorities.

Why China can scale this. The Implementation Opinions sit on top of a regulatory stack that is already operating, including the amended Cybersecurity Law effective January 1, the 748 generative AI service filings completed by end of 2025, and the Anthropomorphic Interactive AI Services Measures taking effect July 15. The dual-track design pairs heavy filing and recall for sensitive sectors with platform-led self-regulation for low-risk consumer scenarios, mirroring the architecture already battle-tested for generative AI and recommendation algorithms.

Global implications. China is the first major jurisdiction to issue agent-specific binding guidance, and the 19 scenarios become a forward-looking taxonomy other regulators will measure against, from EU AI Act agent provisions to UK and Singapore consultations now in public comment. Multinational AI vendors with Chinese enterprise customers will need agent-level documentation specifying decision authority, human override paths, traceability logs, and sector filings before procurement opens summer pilots.
