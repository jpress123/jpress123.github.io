What if the global benchmark for "green AI" was set in Beijing this month and your data-centre roadmap didn't notice?

On May 8, 2026, four central agencies (the NDRC, the National Energy Administration, the MIIT and the National Data Administration) issued the Action Plan to Promote Bidirectional Empowerment of AI and Energy. The document deploys 29 priority tasks across clean-energy supply to AI data centres, compute-power coordination, AI applications across the full energy chain (planning, exploration, production, operations, safety), and a long-term mechanism for sharing high-quality energy datasets. By 2027 the secure, green and economical energy system supporting AI is to be initially built. By 2030, clean-energy supply for AI compute and AI applications in energy are targeted to reach world-leading levels.

Why this matters in China: data-centre and AI-manufacturing electricity already represents over 10 percent of newly added power demand, and four ministries now align project approval, grid planning, industrial standards and data-sharing rules around one bidirectional target. Pilot megawatt-scale AI compute clusters will be co-built with dedicated clean-energy supply. The Dual Control transition from energy-consumption caps to carbon-emission caps gives every provincial grid a measurable AI-compute carbon ledger.

Global impact: Western AI infrastructure is still procured on opportunistic PPAs by individual hyperscalers, while China now anchors a state-coordinated green-AI substrate with binding 2027 and 2030 milestones. Chinese low-carbon AI exports gain certification advantage in EU and ASEAN markets that price embedded carbon, and Western data-centre operators face new comparator pressure on tonnes of CO2 per token served.
