Sino-Signals | May 22, 2026 — Alibaba Builds China's AI Factory From the Silicon Up

When one company owns the chip, the server, the model, and the agent, the price of intelligence stops being set by anyone else.

This week made the vertical-integration thesis concrete. In Hangzhou, Alibaba put a full five-layer AI stack on stage. In the field, State Grid Corporation of China committed gigayuan budgets to put robots on its transmission towers. Both moves show Chinese AI moving from model releases toward owned infrastructure, in software and in the physical world.

## Alibaba Launches Qwen3.7-Max and the In-House Zhenwu M890 AI Accelerator

At the Alibaba Cloud Summit on May 20, 2026, Alibaba launched Qwen3.7-Max, its latest proprietary large language model built as a foundation for AI agents, alongside the in-house Zhenwu M890 AI accelerator designed by subsidiary T-Head and the rack-scale Panjiu AL128 server. Alibaba says Qwen3.7-Max handles agentic coding, complex reasoning, and long-horizon tasks, running autonomously for up to 35 hours and managing more than 1,000 tool calls without performance degradation. The M890 carries 144 GB of on-chip memory and 800 GB per second of inter-chip bandwidth, about three times the performance of the prior 810E, and the Panjiu AL128 packs 128 M890 accelerators into a single rack.

Alibaba Cloud senior vice-president Liu Weiguang framed the company as the only firm in China running all five layers of the AI stack, from chips through agentic cloud, models, model-service platforms, and agentic applications. That vertical control lets Alibaba tune model, server, and silicon together and price inference for a domestic market where Chinese model token usage already runs above United States volume, with a captive base in state data centres and the national AI Plus action.

The global effect is on supplier dependency. A Chinese hyperscaler shipping its own training-grade accelerator narrows the single-supplier reliance that has defined AI infrastructure. If the M890 performs near its claimed specs, Alibaba can price agent workloads against domestic silicon, pressuring cloud margins where Western providers still pay Nvidia list prices.

## State Grid Corporation of China Commits 6.8 Billion Yuan to Procure About 8,500 Power-Grid Robots

State Grid Corporation of China has allocated about 6.8 billion yuan, roughly 1 billion US dollars, in 2026 to buy embodied-intelligence systems, planning to procure around 8,500 robots for substation inspection, transmission-network monitoring, and maintenance in hazardous terrain. The fleet spans more than 600 defined tasks and includes about 5,000 quadruped robot dogs for patrol, 500 humanoid robots for live-line work on ultra-high-voltage lines, and 3,000 dual-arm wheeled robots. Suppliers include Unitree Robotics, Deep Robotics, AgiBot, UBTech Robotics, and Fourier Intelligence.

State Grid operates across 26 of mainland China's 31 provincial-level regions, so one order carries a national footprint. China Southern Power Grid and other utilities are buying similar systems, pushing total sector spending on embodied intelligence above 10 billion yuan, about 1.46 billion US dollars, in 2026. A state utility acting as anchor customer guarantees the volume that moves Chinese robot makers from pilots to production lines.

The world's largest utility becoming a testbed for embodied AI gives Chinese robotics firms paid, repeatable field deployments that Western competitors lack. Field data from substations feeds the same models that power humanoid robots for other sectors, and Guangdong Power Grid already exports inspection robot dogs to markets such as Chile.
