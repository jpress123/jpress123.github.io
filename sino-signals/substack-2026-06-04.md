## China's AI Maps Every Solar and Wind Site Nationwide

Researchers at Peking University and Alibaba's DAMO Academy published a study in Nature in May 2026 that located every utility and rooftop renewable asset in China, 319,972 solar photovoltaic facilities and 91,609 wind turbines across 1,915 counties. A deep-learning model trained on sub-metre satellite imagery processed 7.56 terabytes of data to build the first complete, county-level inventory of any nation's wind and solar fleet. The team showed that coordinating these assets at national scale instead of provincial scale cuts generation variability and reduces curtailment, the wasted clean power that has long been one of China's costliest energy problems.

China runs the world's largest clean-energy base, an estimated 15.4 trillion yuan, about 2.26 trillion US dollars, in output last year. A single national view lets the state pair distant sites, so a cloud over Gansu does not unbalance wind corridors in Inner Mongolia, a coordination move fragmented Western markets cannot make.

No other country holds an asset-level view of its renewable fleet, so China can plan grid expansion and data-center siting on information its competitors lack. The team released its dataset and code on Zenodo, turning a national capability into a template any government could copy as AI electricity demand strains grids from PJM to Europe.

Monday morning: if you run a grid, a utility, or a data-center build-out, ask whether you have asset-level visibility of your own renewable fleet, and fund the satellite-and-AI mapping if you do not.

## Chinese Humanoid Robots Go on Sale as Unitree Clears IPO Review

China's humanoid robot makers moved from trade-show demos to consumer sales in late May and early June 2026. Unitree Robotics opened Asia's first embodied-AI experience store in Shanghai's Jing'an district on May 31, selling its G1 and R1 humanoids and Go2 robot dog, and on June 1 its STAR Market IPO cleared the Shanghai exchange listing committee for a 4.2 billion yuan raise. UBTech's consumer brand UWorld opened JD.com pre-orders for a full-size companion humanoid at a 3,000 yuan deposit, release set for June 30, while AgiBot opens its own Shanghai store on June 13. Domestic shipments reached about 18,000 units in 2025 and are projected at 62,500 this year.

Sector financing hit 68.1 billion yuan in the first quarter of 2026, more than all of 2025. A deep domestic supply chain has lifted the localization rate of key components, which lowers cost and lets manufacturers target 10,000-unit annual runs as early as next year.

China is putting full-size humanoids on public price lists while Tesla Optimus, Figure, and Boston Dynamics still show prototypes, resetting cost and timeline expectations across the field. A listed Unitree with fresh capital can fund model training and a dedicated manufacturing base at a pace private rivals will struggle to match.

Monday morning: benchmark Unitree and UBTech unit prices and delivery timelines against your robotics roadmap, and ask whether your strategy assumes a 2027 product or a 2026 one.
