## China Issues World's First Binding Rules for AI Companion Apps

On April 10 the Cyberspace Administration of China and four other ministries issued Order No. 21, the Interim Measures for the Management of AI Anthropomorphic Interaction Services. It is the first binding national rule governing AI companion and emotional-support apps, and it takes effect July 15, 2026. The rule covers any service that simulates a natural person's personality, thinking, and communication style to provide continuous emotional interaction. Providers must run a minor mode, block virtual-partner and virtual-kin services for minors, intervene when a user signals self-harm by contacting guardians or emergency contacts, and remind users of continuous use every two hours. Apps with 1 million registered users or 100,000 monthly actives must file a security assessment with provincial authorities.

China can enforce this quickly because the same algorithm-filing regime that already governs every recommendation engine in the country absorbs the new rule, with annual review built in. App stores must verify a provider's assessment and filing before listing, and fines reach 200,000 yuan where a user's life or health is harmed.

The global implication is timing. US courts are still litigating whether chatbot makers owe a duty of care after teen-suicide cases tied to Character.AI, while China has turned emotional dependency, sycophancy, and minor protection into legal obligations with penalties. Western regulators now have a working template and a first mover to study.

Monday morning: if you ship a companion, character, or emotional-support feature, map it against these measures this week, because the access date is July 15 and the design mandates are becoming the global floor.

## Unitree Becomes First Embodied AI Firm Cleared for China's Stock Market

On June 1 the Shanghai Stock Exchange listing committee cleared Unitree Robotics for a STAR Market IPO, the first embodied AI company approved for China's A-share market. Unitree plans to raise 4.2 billion yuan to fund robot-model research, new products, and a manufacturing base, and aims to ship up to 20,000 humanoid robots in 2026 after shipping roughly 5,500 last year. The clearance lands amid a commercialization wave where AgiBot passed its 10,000th unit in March and domestic humanoid shipments are projected to reach 62,500 units in 2026, up from about 18,000 in 2025.

Capital is flooding the sector. First-quarter 2026 financing of 68.1 billion yuan exceeded the entire 2025 total, with 50 deals closing in March alone. China's component supply chain for actuators, dexterous hands, and AI compute modules is localizing fast, which cuts unit cost and supports volume manufacturing.

A liquid A-share market for embodied AI gives Chinese humanoid makers a financing engine that US and European rivals, dependent on private rounds, struggle to match at scale. The cost curve that made Chinese EVs and batteries globally dominant is now forming under humanoid robots.
