## NUDT and CAS Institute of Metal Research Achieve First Wafer-Scale P-Type WSi2N4 Growth

A joint team from the National University of Defense Technology and the Chinese Academy of Sciences Institute of Metal Research achieved the first wafer-scale controlled growth of monolayer WSi2N4, a P-type two-dimensional semiconductor, using chemical vapor deposition on a liquid gold and tungsten dual-metal substrate. The team reports single-crystal domain sizes at the sub-millimeter level and growth rates approximately 1,000 times faster than prior literature. Optimized P-type WSi2N4 MOSFETs hold International Technology Roadmap for Semiconductors performance targets down to a 3 nm gate length. The result addresses the structural P-type imbalance, the long-running technical bottleneck where defects in 2D materials produce N-type devices easily but cripple the matched P-type devices any CMOS circuit needs.

China's domestic chip industry treats post-Moore materials as a national priority. NSFC funding is earmarked for next-generation AI compute substrates, and SMIC, Hua Hong, and CXMT are actively scouting alternatives to silicon below 3 nm. Pairing NUDT with the CAS Institute of Metal Research consolidates two of the country's strongest 2D-materials labs under one process recipe. The CVD recipe scales on existing 200 mm wafer infrastructure, so domestic foundries are positioned to begin pilot wafer integration tests inside the next four to six quarters.

TSMC, Samsung, Intel, and IMEC have all backed MoS2 and WSe2 as the dominant 2D candidates for sub-3 nm CMOS. China just placed a different P-type compound in pole position, with hole mobility, on-state current density, and chemical stability competitive with the existing Western roadmaps. Western foundry roadmaps that assume access to a single global 2D-materials toolchain now need to plan for a divergent process stack emerging from China that domestic foundries can adopt without paying any IP toll.

If you run R&D at a foundry or equipment maker, brief your 2D-materials team on the WSi2N4 growth recipe and pull forward your evaluation roadmap by two quarters.

## State Grid Commits 6.8 Billion Yuan to Procure 8,500 Robots in 2026

State Grid Corporation of China announced 2026 procurement of approximately 8,500 embodied AI systems totaling 6.8 billion yuan, roughly $995 million, the largest single-year industrial robot order in the sector's history. The fleet includes 5,000 quadruped robot dogs at a 1.5 billion yuan budget, 500 humanoid robots at 2.5 billion yuan, and dual-arm units assigned to substation patrol, transmission-line maintenance, and high-voltage live-line work. State Grid expects each robot unit to save 500,000 to 800,000 yuan in annual labor costs, with payback inside two to three years and inspection efficiency up roughly 5x. Suppliers include Unitree Robotics, Deep Robotics, AGIBOT, UBTech, and Fourier Intelligence.

State Grid serves more than 1.1 billion people across 26 provinces. Procurement runs in three waves through 2026, pilot in Q1, large-scale order in Q3, and supplementary in Q4. China Southern Power Grid is following with its own program, lifting total Chinese utility-sector embodied AI capex above 10 billion yuan for the year. Domestic humanoid suppliers now have a one-year, 8,500-unit baseload that lets them industrialize unit cost and reliability data inside a single procurement cycle.

Western utility procurement still treats humanoid robots as pilot-scale technology three to five years out. State Grid is contracting at production scale today, and the per-robot economics, with payback inside three years, will set the global benchmark for utility automation business cases. EPRI, ENTSO-E, and US ISO operators will face board questions about why their inspection workforce models still assume human-only field operations.
