What happens when Chinese AI models go from 0% to 30% of global token consumption in a single year?

According to OpenRouter data, Chinese LLMs grew their share of global token usage by 421% between February 2025 and February 2026. Moonshot AI now holds 14.5%, DeepSeek 9.0%, and MiniMax 4.2%. In that same period, Anthropic dropped from 42.2% to 14.7%, Google from 25.8% to 18.8%.

The driver is cost. Chinese open-source models deliver competitive performance at a fraction of the per-token price. DeepSeek-R1 matched frontier benchmarks on math reasoning and code generation at dramatically lower training cost. When enterprises optimize for intelligence-per-dollar, the price advantage compounds and switching becomes sticky.

What this means for global AI strategy:

The global AI model market was a US duopoly 12 months ago. It is now a three-way race. For every enterprise paying Western model providers, a Chinese open-source alternative now exists at 20% to 40% of the cost. This reprices the entire AI infrastructure layer.

Monday morning action: benchmark your current model costs against DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax for non-sensitive workloads. The cost differential is large enough to fund additional AI projects from the savings alone. If you invest in or compete with Western AI providers, recalibrate revenue projections to account for Chinese open-source price pressure that did not exist 12 months ago.
