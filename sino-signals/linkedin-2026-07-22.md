How many of your engineers are already building on Chinese AI models without anyone deciding they should?

On July 20, China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology reported that the country's open-source large models have passed 10 billion cumulative downloads worldwide. The figure came in the government's official half-year industrial report, alongside two more data points: AI adoption now exceeds 30 percent among larger Chinese manufacturers, and national intelligent computing capacity reached 2,185 exaflops, up 177 percent year on year.

Why this matters in China: open weights are a deliberate distribution strategy. Alibaba's Qwen family has overtaken Meta's Llama in cumulative Hugging Face downloads, and DeepSeek, Kimi and GLM ship competitive weights at a fraction of Western training prices. Beijing now tracks global downloads the way it tracks exports, because every download seeds a fine-tune, a course syllabus or a startup built on a Chinese base model.

The global impact: developer mindshare compounds. Universities, research labs and cost-sensitive companies worldwide teach, benchmark and build on Chinese open weights because they are free and strong. That makes Chinese architectures the default substrate everywhere outside the closed US frontier, and Western closed-model vendors are competing against free.

Monday morning action: ask your engineering leads which Chinese open-weight models are already running in your stack, sanctioned or not. Set an evaluation, licensing and data-provenance policy this quarter, so the dependence is a deliberate decision instead of an audit finding.
