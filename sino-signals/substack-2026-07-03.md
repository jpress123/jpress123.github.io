# Sino-Signals | Friday, July 3, 2026 — A Frontier Model for Free, and a Super App That Talks Back

*Zhipu gives away a model within one percent of the US flagship, while Alipay rebuilds a billion-user app around a single AI agent.*

Two moves from June are still reshaping the ground this week. One attacks the price of frontier intelligence, the other rewires how a billion people will reach it. Together they show China competing on distribution and economics, the two places where incumbents feel pain fastest.

## Zhipu Gives Away a Frontier Model That Rivals US Flagships

Zhipu AI released GLM-5.2 on June 17 and published the full weights on Hugging Face and ModelScope under the MIT license, free to download, deploy, and use commercially. The 744-billion-parameter mixture-of-experts model activates 40 billion parameters, carries a one-million-token lossless context window, and scored 81.0 on Terminal-Bench 2.1, up 17.5 points over GLM-5.1. On FrontierSWE it trails Claude Opus 4.8 by one percent while beating GPT-5.5, the highest ranking ever recorded by an open-source model, and it took first place among globally available models in the million-user Code Arena blind test.

China can scale this because the launch was engineered for its own stack. Inference was adapted on day zero to eight domestic chip platforms, including Huawei Ascend, Cambricon, and Moore Threads, so the model runs at scale without Nvidia hardware. Zhipu trades in Hong Kong at a market value above HK$685 billion, up more than 1,200 percent from its IPO price, the balance sheet behind giveaway economics.

Globally, a frontier-class coding model under the most permissive license compresses the price umbrella that US flagship APIs depend on. Early enterprise adopters report cutting monthly model bills by half or more after routing work to GLM-5.2, and Hugging Face underwrote six hours of free global inference at launch, its first such subsidy for any single model. When open weights cover 90 percent of flagship capability, the premium for the last 10 percent shrinks fast.

Monday morning: benchmark GLM-5.2 against your current coding-model spend, and stand up a multi-model routing layer so pricing power stays with the buyer.

## Alipay Rebuilds Itself Around an AI Agent Named Abao

Ant Group released the AI version of Alipay on June 16, built around an agent named Abao, and called it the largest redesign in the app's history and the first super app to complete a full-stack AI transformation. Users swipe right into a conversation box where one sentence commands tens of thousands of services, from booking appliance repair to sending parcels and hailing rides. The new build strips the interface to two pages, Abao and a unified Assets ledger, and is live in invitation-based testing ahead of a gradual rollout.

China can scale this because Alipay's roughly one billion users give an agent instant distribution, and payment, identity, and mini-program rails already sit inside one app, so Abao completes transactions end to end instead of handing off. Tencent is testing a built-in agent for WeChat at the same time, so China's two dominant super apps are converging on the same conversational pattern.

Globally, Western platforms treat AI as a feature bolted onto an existing interface, while Alipay makes the agent the interface itself. If invitation testing converts, PayPal, Apple, and Revolut face a template where conversation replaces the app grid. The design also stakes out an early answer on agentic-payment safety: Abao acts only on explicit user consent, and every fund movement requires personal confirmation.

Monday morning: map which of your services an agent could complete end to end today, and decide where confirmation friction genuinely belongs.
