## CATL Signs the World's Largest Sodium-Ion Battery Order

On April 27, 2026, CATL announced it would deliver 60 gigawatt-hours of sodium-ion batteries to Beijing HyperStrong Technology over three years, the largest sodium-ion order on record. CATL, the world's largest EV and storage battery maker, said the deal proves it has cleared the barriers to mass-producing sodium-ion cells and can now deliver at scale. Davis Zhang of battery supplier Suzhou Hazardtex called it a "DeepSeek moment" for the energy storage industry, since sodium replaces costlier lithium and lowers both material cost and manufacturing complexity.

China can scale this because storage construction is expanding specifically to back AI computing and data centers, and sodium-ion removes the lithium price exposure that has constrained grid storage economics. HyperStrong is one of the country's largest storage integrators, and Chinese producers have separately announced plans for more than 600 GWh of new storage capacity in just the first two months of 2026.

Globally, sodium-ion at scale resets the floor price for grid storage, compounding the cost advantage Chinese developers carry into solar-plus-storage tenders across the Middle East, Southeast Asia, and Europe. It also eases pressure on lithium supply chains, which changes the raw-material risk calculus for every downstream EV and storage program. ## A Nature Study Asks Whether AI Can Repay Its Own Power Bill

On May 2, 2026, researchers led by Kaile Zhou at Hefei University of Technology published a study in Communications Sustainability, a Nature Publishing Group journal, modeling whether AI's grid-optimization savings can offset the electricity that AI data centers consume in China. Across nine scenarios, net electricity saving, defined as saved electricity divided by consumed electricity, peaks at 130.3 percent by 2040, but only under combined high efficiency and high adoption. Net savings then decline about 10 percent from 2050 to 2055 as technology gains saturate, with wide variation across regions.

China can scale this because it coordinates the grid, the data center buildout, and AI deployment policy in one push, including the four-ministry AI Plus Energy Action Plan released in May 2026. The study gives planners a quantified target, balanced regional deployment, that turns AI energy spending into measurable system savings.

Globally, the finding reframes the AI energy debate from a fixed cost into a deployment-efficiency question. A peer-reviewed result showing net savings above 100 percent under disciplined deployment will enter Western grid-planning and AI-permitting debates by late 2026. The regional-variation warning also matters, since poorly sited AI infrastructure can erase the savings entirely. Monday morning, commission a net-electricity-saving analysis for your own grid, and pair every data center siting decision with a grid-efficiency use case so the new load is offset instead of purely additive.
