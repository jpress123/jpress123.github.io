## China Names 51 AI Energy Scenarios to Rewire Its Power System

On May 26, China's National Energy Administration convened a national "AI plus energy" meeting in Shenzhen and published its first list of 51 high-value scenarios for fusing AI into the energy system, from AI-generated grid planning and assessment to virtual power plant operation and vehicle-to-grid coordination. Twenty-five energy companies signed an initiative opening those scenarios to AI developers, and the agency released its China AI plus Energy Development Report 2026. The NEA also quantified the demand side of the equation: China's computing centers consumed 170 billion kWh in 2025, 1.6 percent of national electricity, across 42 ten-thousand-GPU AI clusters, with hub-node compute power demand growing roughly 39.5 percent a year.

China can scale this because the scenario list converts policy into procurement. State-owned grid and energy giants execute NEA mandates quickly, and a 2025 implementation plan already commits to at least five specialized energy AI models and ten replicable demonstration projects by 2027. With 3.99 billion kW of generating capacity, including 1.25 billion kW of solar, China is the largest sandbox on earth for AI-run grid operations.

Globally, the scenarios and standards that emerge will travel with Chinese grid equipment and EV charging infrastructure into export markets. Western utilities still treat AI as a pilot-stage add-on, while China is standardizing it as core grid infrastructure.

## Tencent Builds a WeChat AI Agent for 1.4 Billion Users

Tencent is testing a prototype AI agent embedded directly in WeChat and has made it a top company priority. Users reach the agent by swiping right from the home screen, then issue commands it executes across WeChat's millions of mini programs, such as finding a cafe matching taste and price preferences and ordering the drink. Tencent plans to begin the required regulatory approval process as early as this month, followed by a small external test group before wider rollout.

China can scale this because WeChat already wires together identity, payments, and fulfillment for 1.4 billion users, so the agent inherits a transaction-ready execution layer on day one. The competitive clock is running: Alibaba's Qwen assistant reached 300 million monthly active users on Taobao and opened its agent platform to brands including KFC and Luckin Coffee in early June, while JD.com is reportedly teaming up with Tencent on agent collaboration.

Globally, China's agentic interface war is being fought inside super-apps that own the full transaction loop. Apple, Google, and Meta must stitch agents across fragmented apps and payment rails. Whoever first ships an agent that completes purchases for a billion users will set consumer expectations everywhere.

Monday morning: audit how your products surface when an agent, not a human, does the browsing. Agent-readable menus, prices, and inventory are becoming the new SEO.
