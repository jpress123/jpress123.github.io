What happens when three Chinese ministries decide that Personal Information Protection enforcement should run in parallel across seven sectors at once?

On April 2, the Cyberspace Administration of China, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, and the Ministry of Public Security jointly launched a 2026 Personal Information Protection Special Action Series. The campaign covers apps and SDKs, internet advertising, education, transportation, healthcare, finance, and a dedicated criminal-cases track. Severe non-compliance triggers forced service suspensions.

Why China can move at this scale. Three central agencies acting jointly across seven verticals is the broadest cross-sector PI enforcement pass since the Personal Information Protection Law took effect in 2021. The notice formalizes that organizations cannot make facial recognition the sole verification method when alternatives exist. It mandates parental consent for processing data of users under 14 in schools, after-school training, and healthcare apps, and requires lenders, hospitals, and schools to disclose every third-party data recipient by name, purpose, and method.

Why Western leaders should care. China is now setting an enforcement bar that exceeds GDPR in vertical specificity and pace. The facial recognition rule directly addresses biometric over-collection that GDPR enforcement has struggled to operationalize. EU and California regulators will study the language closely. Multinational operators in China face a compliance cycle measured in weeks, not the multi-year transition periods Western jurisdictions typically grant. Internet advertising rules requiring named disclosure of every downstream PI recipient will reshape global ad-tech graphs that route through Chinese platforms.

Monday morning action. If your organization processes Chinese user data through any of the seven named sectors, commission a gap analysis by mid-May. Prioritize three workstreams, the facial recognition stack, the third-party SDK inventory, and the parental consent documentation trail for users under 14. This is a six-week sprint, not an annual compliance refresh.
