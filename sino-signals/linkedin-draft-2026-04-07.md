China delivered 40,000 autonomous vehicles last year. This year, the target is 100,000. By 2030, 750,000. Is your logistics strategy ready for that cost curve?

China's autonomous delivery sector pulled in nearly 6 billion yuan in funding in 2025. Cainiao merged its fleet into Jiushi Intelligence, creating the country's largest RoboVan fleet at over 20,000 units. Neolix plans to ship 50,000 vehicles in 2026 alone. JD Logistics runs 600+ autonomous vehicles across 30 cities. Hardware costs have compressed to 15,000 to 20,000 yuan per unit, crossing the threshold where unmanned delivery undercuts human couriers on high-density routes.

The scale advantage is structural. China moves 130 billion parcels per year, more than triple the US volume. Cainiao feeds directly into Alibaba's commerce ecosystem. JD serves JD.com, 7Fresh, and Sam's Club. Meituan embeds driverless vehicles into its food delivery network. No Western market combines this parcel density with platform-owned autonomous infrastructure at this level.

When 100,000 autonomous vehicles are running across Chinese cities by year-end, per-parcel costs reset for global e-commerce fulfillment. The technology stack being refined on Chinese roads, L4 navigation, fleet orchestration AI, multi-sensor fusion, will export to Southeast Asia and the Middle East within 12 months. Neolix has already announced 50,000 overseas units for 2027.

If you manage e-commerce fulfillment, benchmark your per-parcel cost against Cainiao's fleet economics. If you lead product strategy for a brand selling into China, factor autonomous delivery windows into your distribution planning. The shift from human couriers to unmanned fleets changes speed guarantees, return logistics, and customer touchpoints.
