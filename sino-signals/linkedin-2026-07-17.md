What happens to your app, your storefront, and your ad budget when the customer stops tapping through screens and just tells an agent what they want?

Ant Group's Alipay has rebuilt its billion-user super app around an AI agent called Abao. Swipe right and a chat interface pays bills, finds EV chargers, checks housing-fund balances, and books services across tens of thousands of real-world scenarios from a single prompt. Invite-only testing opened June 16, with a broader rollout underway. It is the largest redesign in Alipay's history, and every fund transfer still needs explicit user confirmation.

Why this matters in China: Alipay reaches about 1 billion users and already connects to government benefits, utilities, transport, and healthcare, so an agent layer hits national scale the day it ships. Ant owns the full stack from identity to settlement, and WeChat is still deciding how to add agents. That hands Alipay a real opening.

Global impact: Agentic commerce, where the assistant completes the purchase instead of surfacing a link, undercuts the app-and-search economics that Western platforms depend on. A live agent at billion-user scale gives China a working template for the interface that could replace tapping through apps. Banks, retailers, and platform owners now have a concrete model to study.

Monday morning: Map which customer journeys an AI agent could complete end to end, from bill pay to booking, and decide whether you supply that agent or get intermediated by one.
