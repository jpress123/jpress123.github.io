## DeepSeek V4 Will Run Natively on Huawei Ascend 950 PR, Breaking CUDA Dependence

DeepSeek is preparing to deploy V4, a near-trillion-parameter architecture, natively on Huawei's Ascend 950 PR chips. This is not a compatibility patch or a performance compromise. It is a ground-up integration of China's most capable open-weight model with China's most advanced AI processor.

The market has noticed. Alibaba, ByteDance, and Tencent have placed bulk orders for hundreds of thousands of Ascend units, driving prices up roughly 20%. Huawei targets 1.6 million total Ascend dies in 2026 production, with the training-focused Ascend 950DT arriving in Q4. SMIC fabricates the chips at enhanced 7nm.

If DeepSeek stabilizes the full software stack, including compilers, operators, communication libraries, distributed training, and inference frameworks, then China's core model development pipeline becomes structurally independent of CUDA. ## Five Chinese Ministries Mandate AI Curriculum from Primary School Through University

On April 10, China's Ministry of Education and four partner ministries released the "AI + Education" Action Plan. It mandates AI education across all local curriculum systems starting at the primary level and requires universities to offer AI as a public foundational course. AI competency will be included in teacher qualification exams.

China's centralized education system reaches 290 million students. A single ministerial directive reshapes curriculum nationwide within one to two academic years. By embedding AI literacy at the primary level, China is building a generation-wide technical baseline at a speed no market-driven system can match. Within five years, Chinese university graduates will arrive in the global workforce with formal AI training baked into every discipline.
