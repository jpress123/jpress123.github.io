## Huawei Presents the Tau (τ) Scaling Law at IEEE ISCAS in Shanghai

On May 25, 2026, Huawei semiconductor leader He Tingbo presented the Tau Scaling Law at the IEEE International Symposium on Circuits and Systems in Shanghai. The proposal: optimise chips for time, the signal propagation delay across devices and systems, rather than for transistor size. The accompanying LogicFolding architecture folds and stacks logic circuits vertically and at fixed process nodes delivers a 55 percent transistor density increase, 41 percent performance-core energy efficiency gain, 13 percent peak frequency lift, and 30 percent wiring-length reduction. The fall 2026 Kirin SoC is the first commercial implementation at a 3.1 GHz CPU performance core. The 2031 target is 1.4 nanometre equivalent density using legally available manufacturing equipment.

China has already mass-produced 381 chips under the Tau framework over six years, so the announcement is not a paper proposal but a production track record. Western analysts caution that thermal management of 3D stacked logic remains a hard manufacturing problem and that TSMC has been pioneering 3D packaging, chiplet stacking and hybrid bonding for nearly a decade. The strategic point is not whether Tau exceeds TSMC. It is that Tau reframes the contest from lithography access to system-level integration, where Huawei's vertical span gives it advantages Western pure-play chip designers lack.

Source: [Huawei IEEE ISCAS Tau Scaling Law](https://www.huawei.com/en/news/2026/5/ieee-iscas-tau-scaling)

## Robert Wu's Baiguan Essay Maps the Galapagosization

Wu's May 31 essay argues that US export controls have triggered a structural galapagosization of the Chinese tech stack, with Huawei's Tau Scaling and DeepSeek V4 converging on a non-NVIDIA hardware-software ecosystem. Paul Triolo's analysis, quoted in the piece, makes the case that Huawei's vertical integration across telecom, cloud, networking, AI systems and OS becomes strategically valuable under a τ scaling framework, because optimisation happens across the entire stack simultaneously. NVIDIA's Jensen Huang told Dwarkesh Patel in April 2026 that the worst outcome for the United States is an open-source ecosystem running on a foreign tech stack while a closed ecosystem runs on the American one.

Source: [Baiguan / Robert Wu](https://www.baiguan.news/)

## ByteDance Commits Over 5.7 Billion Dollars to Huawei Ascend

ByteDance has reportedly committed more than 5.7 billion US dollars to Huawei Ascend AI chip procurement across 2026, the single largest known order for non-NVIDIA AI accelerators by any global hyperscaler. The order spans Ascend 950 and 950PR class chips at roughly 1 petaflop FP8 with 128 to 144 gigabytes of on-chip memory and 2 terabytes per second interconnect bandwidth. ByteDance anchors the supernode cluster strategy that DeepSeek V4 is co-designed around, translating the abstract galapagosization thesis into concrete annual orders for Chinese fabs and EDA toolchains.

Source: [Huawei Central, ByteDance Ascend procurement](https://www.huaweicentral.com/bytedance-to-order-5-7-billion-huawei-ai-chips-over-nvidia-in-2026/)

## DeepSeek V4 Is the First Chinese Frontier Model Built for Huawei

DeepSeek shipped V4 on April 24, 2026 with a hybrid attention design pairing Compressed Sparse Attention and Heavy Compressed Attention, cutting per-token inference FLOPs to 27 percent of V3.2 and KV cache to 10 percent at the 1 million token context window. Huawei confirmed full support on Ascend 950-based SuperNode clusters and used Ascend for part of V4-Flash training. The bottlenecks on non-NVIDIA hardware (memory bandwidth, data movement, kernel fusion, stack maturity) are precisely the surfaces V4 was engineered to relax.

Source: [Ghacks, DeepSeek V4 with Huawei Ascend Support](https://www.ghacks.net/2026/04/26/deepseek-releases-v4-models-with-9-5x-lower-memory-requirements-and-huawei-ascend-support/)

## Huawei R2C Protocol for Humanoid Robotics

At AW 2026 Huawei laid out the Robot-to-Cloud (R2C) protocol, a distributed computing architecture that splits humanoid AI workloads across robot terminal, edge nodes and cloud. With CANN fused-kernel optimisation on Ascend NPUs, policy-network training convergence dropped from 47 minutes on NVIDIA hardware to 9.3 minutes. China commands roughly 80 percent of global humanoid robot manufacturing as of May 2026, and the MIIT Humanoid and Embodied Intelligence Standard System (HEIS 2026) was drafted with more than 120 institutions and manufacturers. The robotics layer is now galapagosized too.

Source: [EE Times, Humanoid Robots at AW 2026](https://www.eetimes.com/humanoid-robots-exit-labs-mapping-the-technical-path-to-embodied-ai-at-aw-2026/)

## Chindata Lingqiu Solves the Thermal Problem Tau Scaling Creates

Chindata Group's Lingqiu Campus in Shanxi runs a hybrid thermal solution that integrates liquid cold-plate cooling with magnetic levitation phase-change air cooling. China's liquid-cooling market is forecast to grow from 498 million US dollars in 2024 to 3.35 billion by 2032 at 27 percent compound annual growth, with dozens of Chinese listed companies (Sanhua Intelligent Controls, Lansi Technology, Kehua Data, Shenzhen FRD, Goaland Energy Conservation) announcing liquid-cooling expansions. Huawei's LogicFolding architecture compounds heat dissipation at the centre of 3D stacked dies, which is the most-cited reason Western analysts say Tau Scaling will struggle in mass production. Chinese thermal vendors solving the problem at rack and chassis level matter for whether Tau ships at volume.

Source: [SCMP, China liquid cooling AI data centres](https://www.scmp.com/tech/article/3343582/china-moves-deeper-liquid-cooling-ai-data-centres-push-their-thermal-limits)
