When did a humanoid robot last show up on a price list you could actually buy from?

In China, that moment is now. Unitree Robotics opened Asia's first embodied-AI experience store in downtown Shanghai on May 31, selling its G1 and R1 humanoids and Go2 robot dog to walk-in customers. On June 1, its STAR Market IPO cleared the Shanghai exchange listing committee for a 4.2 billion yuan raise. UBTech's consumer brand UWorld opened pre-orders on JD.com for a full-size companion humanoid, 3,000 yuan deposit, with release set for June 30. AgiBot opens its own Shanghai store on June 13. Domestic shipments hit about 18,000 units in 2025 and are projected at 62,500 this year.

Why China can scale this fast: sector financing reached 68.1 billion yuan in the first quarter of 2026, more than all of 2025, with 50 deals closing in March alone. A deep domestic supply chain has pushed up the localization rate of key components, cutting cost and letting manufacturers target 10,000-unit annual runs as early as next year.

For Western leaders, the gap is timing. China is putting full-size humanoids on public price lists while Tesla Optimus, Figure, and Boston Dynamics still show prototypes. A listed Unitree with fresh capital can fund model training, new products, and a manufacturing base at a pace private rivals will struggle to match. Volume across homes, logistics, and factories compounds the lead.

Monday morning: benchmark Unitree and UBTech unit prices and delivery timelines against your own robotics roadmap. If you invest, read Unitree's STAR Market prospectus for the cost structure behind 62,500 projected units. Ask whether your humanoid strategy assumes a 2027 product or a 2026 one.
