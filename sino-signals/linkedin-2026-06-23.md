What happens to your cloud roadmap when a competitor ships the entire agent stack, top to bottom, on chips you cannot buy?

On June 5, Huawei Cloud opened its INSPIRE 2026 conference in Shanghai and introduced Agentic Infra, a full enterprise stack for autonomous AI agents. The lineup includes the AICS compute service running clusters above 100,000 cards at 200 EFLOPS, the AgentArts enterprise agent platform with an open-source edition called openJiuwen, and four Industry AI Foundry zones for smart manufacturing, healthcare, embodied AI, and scientific computing. Huawei also launched an AI Model Partner Program with more than 20 Chinese model makers, including Zhipu AI, DeepSeek, MiniMax, Kimi, and Baidu.

Why this matters in China: Huawei already leads China's financial hybrid cloud market and serves more than 5,500 customers, giving it a captive base to push agents into regulated industries. A homegrown compute, model, and agent stack lets Chinese enterprises run autonomous agents without Western chips or cloud. State policy targeting 1,000 industrial AI agents hands the Smart Manufacturing Zone a ready pipeline of buyers.

Global impact: Huawei is assembling an end-to-end agentic cloud that competes head-on with AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud, built entirely on domestic silicon. Open-sourcing the AgentArts kernel pressures Western vendors who keep agent runtimes proprietary. In any market open to Chinese tech, enterprises now have a full-stack alternative with published performance numbers.
