## National Energy Administration NDRC MIIT and National Data Administration Issue AI Plus Energy Action Plan With 29 Measures Targeting 2030 Green Compute Integration

On May 8, the National Energy Administration, the National Development and Reform Commission, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, and the National Data Administration jointly issued an action plan to promote mutual empowerment of artificial intelligence and energy by 2030. The plan carries 29 measures spanning safe and reliable energy supply for computing infrastructure, green and low-carbon transition for hub data centers, efficient coordination between compute load and the power grid, and high-value AI applications across the energy sector. Operators are directed to lift the green electricity share at computing facilities through green certificate purchases and green power trading market participation, and to replace diesel backup generators with cleaner alternatives.

The action plan slots into the Eastern Data Western Computing build-out, with Qinghai, Xinjiang, and Heilongjiang named as resource-abundant non-hub regions for new compute and renewable co-location. The 29 measures pair compute self-sufficiency with energy self-sufficiency, with State Grid dispatching algorithms, virtual power plant aggregation, and AI-optimized fault forecasting written directly into the application catalogue.
