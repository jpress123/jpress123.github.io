## NIST's CAISI Evaluation Finds DeepSeek V4 Pro Trails the US Frontier by 8 Months While Beating GPT-5.4 mini on Cost in 5 of 7 Benchmarks

On May 1, 2026, NIST's Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) released an independent evaluation of DeepSeek V4 Pro, the 1.6 trillion parameter open-weight model DeepSeek shipped on April 24, 2026. CAISI used 16 benchmarks across 35 models, fitted an Item Response Theory model, and put V4 Pro at an IRT Elo of 800 against GPT-5.5 at 1260, a roughly 8-month lag. V4 Pro is the most capable PRC model CAISI has evaluated to date, scoring 96 percent on PUMaC 2024, 97 percent on OTIS-AIME-2025, and 90 percent on GPQA-Diamond. On long agentic tasks like CTF-Archive-Diamond, V4 Pro dropped more than 30 points behind GPT-5.5.

China can scale this because DeepSeek's open-weight release on Hugging Face and Modelscope reaches every Chinese enterprise integrator on the day of release, and the V4 architecture stack of Compressed Sparse Attention, Heavily Compressed Attention, Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections, and the Muon optimizer was engineered for training larger models on tighter compute. At 1.74 US dollars per million input tokens, the model is deployable inside Chinese banks, hospitals, universities and government clouds at a fraction of US frontier pricing.

The global implication is that NIST has put an official US-government number on a debate previously fought with vendor self-reports. European, Southeast Asian and Gulf procurement teams now have a NIST table to cite when comparing OpenAI, Anthropic and DeepSeek. Western frontier vendors face a public US data point arguing their price-to-capability advantage is shrinking, while DeepSeek's open weights remove the deployment friction that locked some markets into closed US offerings.
