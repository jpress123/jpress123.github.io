## Datang Zhongwei 500MW Solar Powers China's First Large-Scale Computing-Electricity Coordination Direct-Supply AI Data Center Cluster

On May 2, China Datang Corporation placed a 500 MW solar plant in Zhongwei, Ningxia, into commercial operation, the first utility-scale green power installation purpose-built to feed an AI data center cluster under a coordinated computing-electricity (算电协同) framework. The plant pairs with a 1.5 GW wind project slated for full grid connection in September 2026, with dedicated transmission lines delivering photovoltaic power directly to data center halls without circulating through the main grid. First-phase capex is 8.7 billion yuan. The first phase is engineered to supply the 2.29 billion kWh annual demand of the Zhongwei Cloud Base.

The Eastern Data, Western Computing initiative has already concentrated more than 80 percent of national intelligent compute capacity into eight hub nodes in renewable-rich western provinces, and the 80 percent green electricity threshold for new hub data centers gives Datang a regulated path to replicate the Zhongwei model across Inner Mongolia, Gansu, and Guizhou. State Grid and the National Energy Administration backed the 算电协同 model in March 2026 policy guidance. Jinko Power has separately announced a 1 GW direct-connect project running in parallel.

Global implications. Direct renewable-to-data-center pairing at this scale arrives roughly two years ahead of comparable utility-scale deployments planned in the United States and Europe.
