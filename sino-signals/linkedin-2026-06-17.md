What happens to your creative tool stack when a single app ships an entire AI production crew?

Today in Xiamen, Meitu opens its 2026 Image Festival under the theme "Your AI Image Team Is in Position." The Hong Kong listed company (01357) is set to debut and upgrade about eight products, and it is reframing them as task-specific agents that shoot, design, edit, and score as a coordinated team instead of as separate tools. CEO Wu Xinhong credits an internal startup-team mechanism that already produced Wink, Meitu Design Room, Kaipai, and the RoboNeo agent. The stock rose more than six percent on the teaser alone.

Why this matters in China: Meitu reaches hundreds of millions of users across its camera and design apps, so a move from standalone tools to bundled agents lands with a mass consumer base overnight. The festival has launched the company's flagship products every year since 2022, giving Meitu a proven path to convert casual editors into paying subscribers of agentic workflows. Constant demand from short-video and e-commerce creators funds fast iteration.

Global impact: Meitu competes head-on with Adobe, Canva, and Capcut. An agent-team model resets what consumers expect a creative app to do without specialist skill, putting pressure on both price and the all-in-one promise. The approach exports cleanly, since Meitu already earns overseas revenue from English versions of its apps.

Monday morning: Map which steps of your creative production still need a specialist, then ask which a coordinated agent could absorb. Trial an agent stack against your current tool chain on a real campaign to measure the quality and cost gap before competitors set the benchmark.
