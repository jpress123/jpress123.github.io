## China April Lithium Battery Output Hits 235GWh Record as Storage Cells Cross 41.3% Threshold

April 2026 lithium battery production in China reached 235GWh, the highest monthly figure on record, up 7.3 percent month over month and 38.2 percent year over year, according to Gaogong Industry Research (GGII). Energy storage cells accounted for 41.3 percent of total output, breaking the 40 percent threshold for the first time and rising 12.5 percentage points from a year earlier. Power batteries fell to 44.1 percent of mix and consumer cells to 14.6 percent. The convergence between stationary storage and EV battery volumes is the closest in the industry's history.

GGII forecasts 2026 stationary-storage cell shipments above 850GWh, growing more than 35 percent, and overtaking the absolute increment of power batteries for the first time. Lithium carbonate, electrolyte, separator, and anode supply tightened in April, with several materials on temporary allocation. CATL, BYD, EVE, and CALB are running three-shift operations across Ningde, Yibin, Hefei, and Huizhou, while grid-tied storage projects in Inner Mongolia, Xinjiang, and Gansu draw the bulk of new contracts.

The 41.3 percent crossover means China's lithium industrial base now scales to grid storage as fast as it scales to EVs. Western utilities and IPPs procuring four-hour and eight-hour storage face a supply pool that can absorb a multi-hundred-GWh order book without diluting EV throughput. CBAM, IRA domestic-content rules, and tariff regimes will not slow Chinese cell deliveries to the rest of the world, because the same factories produce both EV and storage chemistries.

Lock multi-year storage cell contracts now if you run utility procurement, and write delivery floors into the schedule. Reweight your storage-versus-EV thesis toward storage given the 850GWh shipment trajectory.

## Steel, Cement, and Aluminum Sectors Receive First National ETS Quota Pre-Allocation Under April 10 Deadline

Under MEE Climate Office Letter 2026 No. 32, provincial ecological environment authorities completed pre-allocation of 2025 carbon emission allowances to steel, cement, and aluminum smelting key emitters by April 10, 2026. The threshold for inclusion is 26,000 tonnes of CO2 equivalent in annual direct emissions. Final quota verification follows by September 20, allocation by September 30, and compliance settlement by December 31, 2026. The notice also brings petrochemicals, chemicals, flat glass, copper smelting, paper, and civil aviation under reporting requirements ahead of full ETS inclusion.

China's national ETS now covers an additional roughly 1,500 companies and approximately 3 billion tonnes of CO2 equivalent, lifting market coverage to about 60 percent of national emissions, the largest carbon market in the world. Phase 1 through 2026 uses output-based intensity benchmarks; Phase 2 from 2027 tightens toward an absolute cap. The same MEE platform handles monthly data attestation and quarterly verification, giving Beijing a unified emissions-data backbone across heavy industry that no other jurisdiction operates at this scale.

EU CBAM importers can now point to a domestic Chinese carbon price for steel, cement, and aluminum, narrowing the pass-through penalty on Chinese exporters once the price differential closes. Western producers lose the argument that Chinese heavy industry is uncovered by carbon pricing, which reshapes the WTO subsidy debate and the negotiation posture for any US-EU-China carbon-club arrangement. Korea, Indonesia, and Vietnam will cite this rollout in their own 2026 to 2027 reforms.

If you import steel, cement, or aluminum from China into the EU, request your supplier's 2025 ETS quota and verified emissions report this week, because that document now reduces your CBAM certificate liability.
