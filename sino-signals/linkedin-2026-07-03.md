What happens to frontier AI pricing when a model within one percent of the best US flagship becomes free to download?

Zhipu AI open-sourced its flagship GLM-5.2 on June 17 under the MIT license, the most permissive available, with full weights on Hugging Face and ModelScope. The 744-billion-parameter mixture-of-experts model activates 40 billion parameters, holds a one-million-token context window, and scored 81.0 on Terminal-Bench 2.1, up 17.5 points over its predecessor. On FrontierSWE it trails Claude Opus 4.8 by one percent and beats GPT-5.5, the highest ranking ever for an open-source model. It also took first place among globally available models in the million-user Code Arena blind test.

Why this matters in China: inference was adapted on day zero to eight domestic chip platforms, including Huawei Ascend and Cambricon, so the model scales without Nvidia hardware. Zhipu trades in Hong Kong at a market value above HK$685 billion, up more than 1,200 percent from its IPO price, giving it the balance sheet to sustain giveaway economics. Hugging Face underwrote six hours of free global inference at launch, its first such subsidy for any single model.

Global impact for Western leaders: a frontier-class coding model under an MIT license compresses the price umbrella that US flagship APIs depend on. Early enterprise adopters report cutting monthly model bills by half or more. Open-source alternatives at 90 percent of flagship capability now cover most commercial workloads.
